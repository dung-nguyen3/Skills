### CRITICAL: Merged Cell Border Behavior

When merging cells in openpyxl, only the top-left cell retains styling (borders, colors, etc.). Other cells become MergedCell objects and lose formatting.

**CORRECT PATTERN (always use):**
1. Apply styling (borders, colors, fonts) to ALL cells in range FIRST
2. THEN merge the cells

**Example:**
```python
# Step 1: Style ALL cells in the range
for col_idx in range(start_col, end_col + 1):
    cell = ws.cell(current_row, col_idx)
    apply_cell_style(cell, text=value if col_idx==start_col else '', bg_color=color)

# Step 2: THEN merge
ws.merge_cells(start_row=current_row, start_column=start_col,
               end_row=current_row, end_column=end_col)
```

#### SECTION 4: PHYSICAL EXAM

##### 4a. SPINE - INSPECTION
```
Patient Position: Patient standing, back to examiner with gown open

VERBALIZATION: "I am inspecting for spinal alignment, lateral curvature, and shoulder and iliac crest symmetry."

Items to inspect:
- Skin: lesions, ecchymosis, rashes
- Spine: alignment, lateral curvature, hyper/hypolordosis, kyphosis
- Shoulders: asymmetry
- Iliac crests: asymmetry
- Forward bend test: abnormal spinal curvature
```

##### 4b. SPINE - PALPATION
```
VERBALIZATION: "I am palpating the cervical spine, thoracic spine, lumbar spine, and paraspinal musculature for tenderness and deformity."

Technique: Palpate vertebral processes and spinal musculature starting at base of skull to top of sacrum

What to palpate:
- Cervical spine (C1-C7)
- Thoracic spine (T1-T12)
- Lumbar spine (L1-L5)
- Paraspinal musculature
```

##### 4c. SPINE - RANGE OF MOTION
```
VERBALIZATION: "I am assessing range of motion of the cervical and thoracolumbar spine."

CERVICAL SPINE:
- Flexion: "Bend head forward with chin to chest"
- Extension: "Bend head backward"
- Lateral bending: "Bend head to one side, trying to touch ear to shoulder"
- Rotation: "Rotate head to look over shoulder while keeping rest of spine straight"

THORACOLUMBAR SPINE (stabilize pelvis):
- Flexion: "Bend forward at the hips"
- Extension: "Bend backward at the hips"
- Lateral bending: "Bend to the side like picking up a suitcase"
- Rotation: "Rotate to look behind you using your entire back"
```

##### 4d. LOWER EXTREMITY - INSPECTION
```
VERBALIZATION: "I am assessing the hips, knees, ankles and toes for asymmetry, malalignment, deformities, leg length discrepancy, varicosities, swelling, discoloration, and hair distribution."
```

##### 4e. LOWER EXTREMITY - PALPATION
```
Verbalize landmarks for each body part:

Pelvis/Hips: "I am palpating the anterior superior iliac spine and greater trochanter."
Upper Leg: "I am palpating the quadriceps and hamstrings."
Knee: "I am palpating the patella and the medial and lateral joint lines."
Knee (tendons): "I am palpating the patellar tendon and the quadriceps tendon."
Popliteal fossa: "I am palpating the popliteal fossa for the popliteal pulse, swelling, aneurysm, cyst, and lymphadenopathy."
Lower Leg: "I am palpating the tibia and fibula."
Gastrocnemius: "I am palpating the gastrocnemius for palpable cords and asymmetry."
Soft tissues: "I am palpating the lower extremity for edema."
Ankle: "I am palpating the Achilles tendon and calcaneus." / "I am palpating the medial and lateral malleoli."
Foot: "I am palpating the metatarsals and phalanges." / "I am palpating the MTP joints and IP joints."
```

##### 4f. NEUROLOGIC EXAM
```
GAIT:
VERBALIZATION: "I am observing the patient's gait for asymmetry, weakness and foot drop."
- Casual walk: Observe gait and posture
- Walk on heels: Assesses L4-L5 (dorsiflexion)
- Walk on toes: Assesses S1 (plantar flexion)
- Heel-to-toe (tandem): Balance assessment

STRENGTH TESTING:
VERBALIZATION: "I am assessing strength bilaterally."
- Hip flexion (L1-L2): "Lift your knee up as I try to press it down"
- Hip abduction: "Push your legs outward against my resistance"
- Hip adduction: "Push your legs inward against my resistance"
- Knee flexion (L5-S1): "Pull your leg in against my resistance"
- Knee extension (L3-L4): "Kick your leg out against my resistance"
- Ankle dorsiflexion (L4-L5): "Pull your foot up towards the ceiling against my hand"
- Ankle plantar flexion (S1-S2): "Push down against my hand like your foot is on the accelerator"

REFLEXES (compare bilaterally, assess for hyper/hyporeflexia):
- Patellar (L3-L4): Strike patellar tendon, observe knee extension
- Achilles (S1-S2): Strike Achilles tendon, observe plantar flexion
- Babinski (UMN): Stroke heel to 5th toe then across ball of foot (makes "7")
  - Normal: flexion of toes
  - Abnormal (positive): fanning of toes
```

##### 4g. SPECIALIZED TESTS
```
STRAIGHT LEG RAISE (SLR):
With pt supine, lift leg off table until pain elicited. Note angle. May dorsiflex foot.
SEATED OPTION: Extend knee, evaluate for backward movement, pain, paresthesia into LE.
POSITIVE: Radiating pain down leg ‚Üí lumbosacral/sciatic radiculopathy

CROSSED SLR:
Perform SLR on asymptomatic leg.
POSITIVE: Pain in symptomatic leg when raising contralateral leg = highly specific for disc herniation

SPURLING MANEUVER:
With pt seated/standing, position pt to rotate and extend head. Apply mild compression at top of head.
POSITIVE: Pain/paresthesia into upper extremity ‚Üí cervical radiculopathy

FABER/PATRICK TEST:
Pt supine, position leg in figure-four (flexion, abduction, external rotation of hip).
Place one hand at contralateral ASIS and other hand on ipsilateral knee.
POSITIVE: Reproduced pain ‚Üí hip or SI joint pathology
```

##### 4h. SENSORY DERMATOMES
```
VERBALIZATION: "Close your eyes. Tell me when you feel me touch you and if it feels sharp or dull."

L4: Knee, medial shin (anterior leg)
L5: Anterolateral shin, dorsal foot, great toe
S1: Posterior leg, lateral/plantar foot
Saddle area (S2-S4): EMERGENCY - perineal sensory changes suggest Cauda Equina Syndrome
```

#### SECTION 6: RED FLAGS / WHEN TO RETURN
```
‚ö†Ô∏è RED FLAGS - EMERGENCY SIGNS
Cauda Equina Syndrome: Saddle paresthesia, bowel/bladder dysfunction, bilateral LE weakness, decreased anal sphincter tone
Myelopathy (UMN signs): Hyperreflexia, Babinski (up-going toes), clonus, hypertonia/spasticity, Lhermitte sign
Infection/Cancer: Fever, night sweats, unexplained weight loss, immunosuppression, IV drug use, recent infection

WHEN TO RETURN

- Fever or other systemic symptoms
```
