üîó Most Important Drug Comparisons to Remember

  • Calcineurin Inhibitors: Tacrolimus > Cyclosporine (more effective, less toxic) - both cause nephrotoxicity but NO bone marrow suppression
  • Sirolimus vs Others: ONLY immunosuppressant with NO renal toxicity - ideal for kidney transplants
  • Bone Marrow Suppression: Cytotoxic drugs (azathioprine, methotrexate, cyclophosphamide), mycophenolate, sirolimus ALL suppress marrow. Calcineurin inhibitors (cyclosporine, tacrolimus) do NOT.
  • Azathioprine + Allopurinol: FATAL interaction - must reduce azathioprine dose significantly or avoid completely
  • Cyclophosphamide Hemorrhagic Cystitis: MUST use MESNA + hydration to prevent - acrolein metabolite damages bladder
  • Pregnancy Contraindications: Methotrexate (fetal death) and Mycophenolate (45-49% pregnancy loss)
  • CYP3A4 Interactions: Cyclosporine and Tacrolimus - NO grapefruit juice, watch rifampin (decreases), erythromycin/ketoconazole (increases)
  • Biologics Nomenclature: -momab (mouse), -ximab (chimeric), -zumab (humanized), -umab (human)
  • Basiliximab: Best tolerated biologic - very few adverse effects, used for transplant induction, reduces rejection 50%
  • G-CSF vs EPO: G-CSF increases neutrophils (bone pain common), EPO increases RBCs (needs iron, causes HTN/clots)
  • Corticosteroid Timing: Anti-inflammatory effect is RAPID, immunosuppressive effect takes WEEKS to develop
  • Long-term Steroid Effects: Cushing's syndrome - buffalo hump, moon face, osteoporosis, hyperglycemia, suppressed immunity

üí° Clinical Decision-Making Pearls

  • If kidney transplant ‚Üí Then Sirolimus (NO renal toxicity) or Tacrolimus (most effective)
  • If patient on allopurinol ‚Üí Then AVOID azathioprine (fatal blood dyscrasias)
  • If using cyclophosphamide ‚Üí Then MUST give MESNA + hydration (prevent hemorrhagic cystitis)
  • If transplant induction needed ‚Üí Then Basiliximab (well-tolerated, reduces rejection 50%)
  • If pregnant or planning pregnancy ‚Üí Then AVOID methotrexate and mycophenolate (teratogenic)
  • If renal toxicity develops ‚Üí Then switch from cyclosporine/tacrolimus to sirolimus
  • If gingival hyperplasia develops ‚Üí Then consider tacrolimus over cyclosporine (less gingival effects)
  • If chemotherapy-induced neutropenia ‚Üí Then G-CSF (Filgrastim) - monitor ANC, expect bone pain
  • If chemotherapy-induced anemia ‚Üí Then EPO + iron supplementation - monitor BP and thrombosis risk
  • If patient taking cyclosporine or tacrolimus ‚Üí Then NO grapefruit juice (CYP3A4 inhibition)
  • If rejection episode occurs ‚Üí Then tacrolimus can rescue or use cyclophosphamide for severe cases
  • If TNF-alpha blocker used ‚Üí Then monitor for infections (27-80% risk with adalimumab/etanercept)
  • If using erythropoietin ‚Üí Then MUST supplement iron (needed for RBC production)
  • If T-cell suppression without marrow toxicity needed ‚Üí Then calcineurin inhibitors (cyclosporine/tacrolimus)
  • If multi-drug transplant regimen ‚Üí Then typically tacrolimus + mycophenolate + prednisone (triple therapy)

üè• Organ Transplant Medication Summary

Standard Multi-Drug Approach:

  • Induction (at time of transplant): Basiliximab - reduces rejection 50%, minimal side effects
  • Early Post-Transplant (Most Aggressive): Tacrolimus + Mycophenolate + Prednisone (triple therapy)
  • Maintenance Therapy: Continue immunosuppression to prevent rejection - typically lower doses
  • Rejection Episodes: Tacrolimus can rescue, or use cyclophosphamide for severe cases
  • Special Consideration for Kidney Transplant: Consider Sirolimus (NO renal toxicity) as part of regimen

Timeline:

  • Pre-transplant: Sometimes induction therapy to pre-suppress immune response
  • Day of transplant: Basiliximab + begin triple therapy
  • First 2-3 months: Most aggressive immunosuppression with corticosteroids
  • Long-term: Maintenance with T-cell suppressants ¬± antiproliferatives, taper steroids

üìö Final Key Points for Exam Success

  • NO Bone Marrow Suppression: Calcineurin inhibitors (cyclosporine, tacrolimus), corticosteroids, biologics
  • YES Bone Marrow Suppression: All cytotoxic drugs, mycophenolate, sirolimus
  • Renal Toxicity Ranking: Cyclosporine > Tacrolimus >> Sirolimus (NONE)
  • Most Effective for Transplant: Tacrolimus (widely used drug of choice by most programs)
  • Best Tolerated Biologic: Basiliximab (very few adverse effects, only nausea/GI)
  • MUST Use MESNA: Cyclophosphamide (prevents hemorrhagic cystitis from acrolein)
  • Fatal Drug Interaction: Azathioprine + Allopurinol (reduce dose or avoid)
  • Pregnancy Category X: Methotrexate (fetal death), Mycophenolate (45-49% loss)
  • CYP3A4 Metabolism: Cyclosporine, Tacrolimus (NO grapefruit, watch drug interactions)
  • Requires Iron Supplementation: Erythropoietin (needs iron for RBC production)
  • Cushing's Syndrome: Long-term corticosteroid use (buffalo hump, moon face, osteoporosis)
  • General Immunosuppression Risk: ALL drugs increase infection risk and malignancy risk
