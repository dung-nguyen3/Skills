FOR PATELLOFEMORAL PAIN SYNDROME (PFPS):
  • History: Anterior knee pain worse with stairs, squatting, prolonged sitting ("theater sign")
  • Inspection: Patellar tracking during squat (J-sign)
  • Palpation: Retropatellar tenderness, patellar facet tenderness
  • Patellar Apprehension Test: Push patella laterally - patient apprehensive
  • Patellar Grind Test: Push patella down, have patient contract quad - pain/crepitus
  • Functional: Single-leg squat, step-down test reproduces pain
  • Q-angle: Assess patellar alignment
FOR PLANTAR FASCIITIS:
  • History: Sharp heel pain with first steps in morning
  • Inspection: Foot alignment, arch height
  • Palpation: Exquisite tenderness at medial calcaneal tubercle (origin of plantar fascia)
  • Windlass Test: Passive great toe dorsiflexion reproduces heel pain
  • Ankle ROM: Check for tight Achilles (limited dorsiflexion)
  • Gait: Antalgic, avoiding heel strike
FOR FAIS (Femoroacetabular Impingement):
  • History: Deep groin pain in athlete, C-sign (hand cups anterior hip)
  • FADIR Test (MOST SENSITIVE): Flexion + ADduction + Internal Rotation - reproduces groin pain
  • FABER Test: Flexion + ABduction + External Rotation - may reproduce pain
  • ROM: Limited internal rotation and flexion
FOR GTPS (Greater Trochanteric Pain Syndrome):
  • History: Lateral hip pain over bony prominence
  • Inspection: Trendelenburg gait possible
  • Palpation: Exquisite tenderness over greater trochanter
  • Trendelenburg Test: Single leg stance - pelvis drops on opposite side (weak hip abductors)
  • FABER Test: May reproduce lateral hip pain
  • Resisted Hip Abduction: Weak and painful
FOR BAKER'S CYST:
  • History: Fullness/pressure behind knee
  • Inspection: Visible swelling in popliteal fossa
  • Palpation: Palpable mass in popliteal fossa, soft/fluctuant
  • Foucher's Sign: Mass softens with knee flexion to 45Â° (communicates with joint)
  • Transillumination: Positive (fluid-filled)
  • Knee Exam: Look for underlying pathology (meniscus, OA)
FOR KNEE BURSITIS:
  • Prepatellar: Swelling anterior to patella, "housemaid's knee"
  • Pes Anserine: Tenderness medial knee below joint line (where sartorius, gracilis, semitendinosus insert)
  • Inspection: Localized swelling (prepatellar)
  • Palpation: Fluctuant, tender, warm if inflamed
  • CRITICAL: If warm/erythematous - aspirate to rule out septic bursitis before steroid injection
FOR OCD (Osteochondritis Dissecans):
  • History: Adolescent athlete, vague knee ache, occasional locking/catching
  • Inspection: Effusion possible
  • Palpation: Tenderness over affected condyle (usually medial femoral condyle)
